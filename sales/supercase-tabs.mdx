## The Five Tabs

Once your Supercase is generated, you'll see five content tabs. Each one is a section of the business case — fully editable, AI-generated from your POV, qualification data, and research.

---

### Executive Summary

The **CFO one-pager**. A 3-slide deck structured as a McKinsey Pyramid:

1. **The situation** — What's happening at this company and why it matters now
2. **The complication** — What's at stake if they don't act (Cost of Inaction)
3. **The resolution** — How the three options compare (Do Nothing, DIY, Vendor)

Key KPI boxes at the top show the headline numbers: Cost of Inaction, DIY Net Impact, and Vendor Net Impact.

<Tip>This is the page your champion will screenshot and put in a Slack message to their CFO. Make sure the numbers and narrative are tight.</Tip>

---

### Problem Analysis

A single content block covering the **problem and recommended fix**. This connects the customer's specific pain to your solution narrative.

Edit this to match the language your champion actually uses — the closer it sounds to their words, the easier it is for them to present internally.

---

### Cost of Inaction

Two parts:

1. **COI Narrative** — A written explanation of what inaction costs, in plain language
2. **DIHAP Calculator** — The financial model. Shows quarterly impact projections across confidence bands (P10 through P90) using Monte Carlo simulation

This is where the math lives. The narrative tells the story; the calculator proves it.

<Note>See [Cost of Inaction (COI)](/sales/cost-of-inaction) for a deep dive on the underlying model — S0/S1, adoption rates, and how the simulation works.</Note>

---

### DIY

What happens if the customer solves this themselves. Four sub-tabs:

| Sub-tab | What It Shows |
|---------|--------------|
| **Impact Analysis** | Financial impact simulation for the DIY scenario |
| **Approach & Risks** | Implementation narrative — what they'd need to build, the risks and consequences |
| **Timeline** | Milestones and delivery roadmap for a DIY effort |
| **Budget** | Line-item cost breakdown for building it in-house |

---

### Vendor

What happens if the customer goes with your solution. Same four sub-tabs:

| Sub-tab | What It Shows |
|---------|--------------|
| **Impact Analysis** | Financial impact simulation for the Vendor scenario |
| **Approach & Risks** | Implementation narrative — what deployment looks like, what differentiates this from DIY |
| **Timeline** | Milestones and delivery roadmap with your solution |
| **Budget** | Line-item cost breakdown including license, implementation, and ongoing costs |

---

## Editing Content

Every content block is a rich text editor. You can:

- Edit text directly — click into any section and type
- Let the AI regenerate — use the regenerate button to get a fresh draft incorporating your latest qualification data
- The AI Coach scores your content on the same 0-100 rubric used in Qualify (see [Qualify & AI Coach](/sales/qualify))

<Tip>The best Supercases are a collaboration between AI and seller. Let the AI draft, then add the details only you know from your conversations.</Tip>

## The Three Options Structure

Across all five tabs, the underlying structure is always the same comparison:

- **Do Nothing** — The cost of inaction (what they lose by staying put)
- **DIY** — Honest assessment of the self-build option (acknowledges it works, just not as well)
- **Vendor** — Your solution (differentiated by adoption speed and ceiling, not by magic)

This three-option framing is deliberate. By acknowledging that DIY is a real option, you build trust. By showing the financial gap between DIY and Vendor, you make the case without overselling.
