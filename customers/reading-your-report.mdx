## Key Numbers Explained

### Year 1 Impact
The cumulative financial impact over the first four quarters. This accounts for the **ramp-up period** - adoption doesn't happen overnight, so Year 1 is typically lower than the steady-state run rate.

### Year 2 Run Rate
The annual impact once adoption has fully ramped. This is the **steady-state value** - what you can expect every year going forward.

### Total Impact (Gross)
The cumulative impact over the full time horizon. This is the headline number before costs are subtracted.

### Net Impact
Total impact minus all costs (license fees, implementation, internal effort). This is the bottom-line value.

### Confidence Ranges
Every number comes with a range:

| Band | What It Means |
|------|--------------|
| **Conservative (P10-P25)** | The outcome if things don't go perfectly - adoption is slower, the metric improvement is smaller |
| **Expected (P50)** | The most likely outcome based on the inputs |
| **Optimistic (P75-P90)** | The outcome if everything goes well - strong adoption, full metric improvement |

<Tip>The conservative number is the one to anchor on with your finance team. If the business case works even at P25, it's a strong case.</Tip>

## Where the Numbers Come From

Every input in the model has a source:

- **Your current metrics** (S0) came from your CRM data, reported metrics, or what your team shared during discovery
- **The target metrics** (S1) were set based on what's realistically achievable for your organization
- **The financial baseline** (M0) was derived from your revenue, pipeline, or budget data
- **Adoption rates** reflect typical implementation outcomes, adjustable based on your situation

<Note>You can challenge any input. If a number doesn't match your reality, ask your rep to update it - the entire model recalculates instantly.</Note>

---

## What's the Payback Period?

Look at where the cumulative impact line crosses the cumulative cost line. That crossover point is when the investment has paid for itself. Everything after that is net positive value.
