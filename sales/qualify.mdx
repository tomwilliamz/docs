## What Qualify Does

Qualify is where you organize everything you learned in discovery into a structured format that powers the business case. Think of it as the bridge between your customer conversations and the final Supercase output.

Each section captures a different dimension of the deal. The more specific and complete your inputs, the better the generated content.

---

## The Five Sections

### 1. Corporate Initiative

Connect the problem to something the company is already investing in.

- **Select a strategic initiative** from the research results (earnings calls, press releases, annual reports) or enter one manually
- **Write a connector narrative** explaining how solving this problem directly supports that initiative

<Tip>Tying your business case to a funded initiative means the budget already exists. This is the single most powerful thing you can do for deal velocity.</Tip>

### 2. Problem We Solve

Define the problem with specificity using SPICED criteria:

- **Problem statement** — What's broken? Be specific to this company.
- **Critical event** — What's forcing urgency? Include dates if possible.
- **Prior attempts** — What have they already tried? Why didn't it work?

### 3. Root Cause Insight

Go deeper than symptoms. What's the actual root cause?

This is often the hardest section to fill in well — and the most valuable. A business case built on symptoms gets challenged. One built on root causes gets funded.

### 4. Cost of Inaction

Frame the urgency in financial terms.

- **Narrative** — What happens if they do nothing for 12 more months?
- **Case study** — Select a relevant case study that illustrates the cost of delay

### 5. Stakeholder Mapping

Map the people involved in the decision.

- Assign real names to each role (champion, economic buyer, technical evaluator)
- Identify each stakeholder's stance (supporter, neutral, blocker)
- Flag who your champion is

## AI Coach

Every section has an **AI Coach** available in a right-side drawer. The coach:

- **Scores your input** on a 0-100 scale
- **Gives specific feedback** on what's strong and what's missing
- **Suggests improvements** with actionable next steps

The coach evaluates automatically as you work. Scores update in the sticky navigation so you can see your progress across all five sections at a glance.

### What Gets Rewarded

- Content that mentions the company by name and references their specific situation
- Filled-in fields (completeness matters — empty fields are the biggest deduction)
- Specific dates, dollar amounts, and named stakeholders

### What Doesn't Get Penalized

- Missing executive sponsors you haven't identified yet
- Unverified claims (you're building a hypothesis, not a legal brief)
- Lack of budget confirmation

---

## Scoring Rubric

All sections use the same 0-100 scale with five bands:

| Score | Band | What It Means |
|-------|------|--------------|
| **90-100** | Excellent | Specific to this company, complete, well-connected to business context |
| **70-89** | Good | Solid content with room for more specificity |
| **50-69** | Developing | Content exists but reads as generic — could apply to any company |
| **25-49** | Needs Work | Sparse or vague — multiple fields empty or one-sentence answers |
| **0-24** | Missing | Empty or completely generic |

<Note>Default AI-generated content starts at roughly 50 (Developing). That's the baseline. Your job is to add the company-specific details that push it toward 85+. A well-executed Supercase should score 85 or higher across all sections.</Note>

### What Each Section Looks For

| Section | Key Evaluation Criteria |
|---------|----------------------|
| **Corporate Initiative** | Initiative selected + connector narrative that's specific to the company |
| **Problem** | All fields filled, company-specific language, critical event with dates, prior attempts described |
| **Root Cause** | Goes beyond symptoms, specific to this company's circumstances |
| **Cost of Inaction** | Narrative quantifies impact (dollars, time, risk), case study selected |
| **Stakeholders** | Real names assigned, stances identified, champion flagged |

## From Qualify to Supercase

Once you've filled in your qualification data:

1. Review your scores — aim for 70+ across all sections before generating
2. Click **Generate Supercase** (or **Regenerate** if content already exists)
3. Content generation uses your qualification inputs plus research data to produce tailored output
4. You land on the Executive Summary tab with a draft that reflects your actual discovery

The qualification data doesn't disappear after generation — it stays editable. You can refine your inputs and regenerate as the deal evolves.
