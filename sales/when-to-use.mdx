Supercase isn't just a tool you pull out at the end of a deal. It's designed to evolve with the conversation — from a rough strawman in early discovery through to a polished artifact your champion uses to sell internally when you're not in the room.

---

## Early Stage: The Strawman

**When:** After the first discovery call, before you have deep data.

**What you do:** Kick off a Supercase with just the company domain and a rough addressable revenue number. Let the AI research the company and generate a first draft.

**Why it works:**
- Shows the prospect you've done your homework before the second call
- Gives you a financial framework to structure the next conversation around
- The numbers will be wrong — and that's the point. Wrong numbers invite correction, and correction is engagement.

<Tip>A strawman with approximate numbers gets more engagement than a perfect deck sent three weeks late. Use it to start the conversation, not end it.</Tip>

**What to share:** Nothing yet. This is your internal prep tool. Use the numbers to guide your next discovery questions.

---

## Mid Stage: Collaborate with Your Champion

**When:** You've had 2-3 calls, identified a champion, and have real discovery data.

**What you do:** Use the **Dry Run** path. Fill in the Qualify sections with what you've learned — the real problem, the root cause, who the stakeholders are. Let the AI Coach score your discovery. Then generate the Supercase with tailored content.

**Why it works:**
- The Qualify rubric forces you to pressure-test your discovery: Do you actually know the problem? Have you identified the root cause? Do you know who the economic buyer is?
- Sharing the draft with your champion turns it into a **collaborative document**. They correct your assumptions, add internal context, and start owning the narrative.
- Every correction makes the business case stronger and harder for them to dismiss — they helped build it.

**What to share:** Walk through the Executive Summary and Cost of Inaction with your champion on a call. Ask them to challenge the numbers. Update in real time.

---

## Late Stage: Arm Your Champion

**When:** Your champion needs to take the business case to their CFO, VP, or buying committee — and you won't be in the room.

**What you do:** Polish the Supercase. Make sure every number is defensible. Review each tab:
- **Executive Summary** — Is it specific to this company? Does it connect to a funded initiative?
- **Cost of Inaction** — Are the S0 and S1 values sourced from their data?
- **Three Options** — Does the comparison feel honest? (If DIY looks obviously bad, you've lost credibility.)
- **DIY vs. Vendor** — Are the timelines and budgets realistic?

Export as PDF or share via link.

**Why it works:**
- Your champion doesn't need to build a business case from scratch — it's already built
- The three-option structure (Do Nothing, DIY, Vendor) preempts the "why not build it ourselves?" objection
- Confidence ranges give the CFO the conservative number they need to feel safe
- Every input traces back to something real — there's no hidden multiplier to attack

<Note>The business case your champion presents IS the business case. Not a slide deck about your product — a financial analysis of their problem. That's a fundamentally different conversation with a CFO.</Note>

---

## The Supercase Lifecycle

```
Early Stage          Mid Stage              Late Stage
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Strawman         →   Collaborate         →  Arm Champion

Kickoff with        Dry Run + Qualify       Polish + Export
rough numbers       with real discovery     PDF or share link

Internal prep       Shared with champion    Champion presents
tool                on a call               to buying committee
```

Each stage builds on the last. The strawman becomes the collaborative draft becomes the final deliverable. You're never starting from scratch.
