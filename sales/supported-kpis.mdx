Supercase ships with **15 pre-built KPI types** across four categories. Each is fully wired into the engine, has its own panel inputs, scenario rules, and unit handling. Org admins can also configure additional custom KPIs.

## Go-to-Market

| KPI | What It Measures | Typical S0 | Impact Driver |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Win Rate** | Deal close percentage | 15–30% | Pipeline conversion |
| **Churn Rate** | Annual logo or revenue churn | 5–20% | ARR preservation |
| **Net Dollar Retention** | Expansion vs. contraction | 95–115% | ARR growth |
| **SQLs Generated** | Sales-qualified leads per period | Varies | Pipeline volume |
| **Pipeline Value** | Total pipeline dollars | Varies | Revenue opportunity |
| **GTM Efficiency** | Marketing / sales ROI | Varies | Cost savings |
| **Deal Velocity** | Days to close | 30–120 days | Cash flow acceleration |

## Productivity

| KPI | What It Measures | Typical S0 | Impact Driver |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Tickets Resolved** | Support volume handled | Varies | Operational throughput |

| **Onboarding Time** | Time to make a new hire productive | 60–180 days | Faster ramp, lower cost-per-hire |

## Structural and Risk

These KPIs use a **step-change** model: impact arrives at go-live, not on an adoption curve. The savings are structural rather than behavior-driven.

| KPI | What It Measures | Typical Use |
|---|---|---|
| **Structural Takeout** | Replacing a legacy vendor or platform | "Retire SAP Ariba" — savings equal the replaced contract |
| **Risk Reduction** | Probability × cost of a controllable event | Compliance, security, fraud, downtime |
| **Consumption Waste** | Unused capacity or inefficient consumption | Cloud spend, licenses, materials |

## Special Behaviors

### Compounding KPIs: Churn and NDR

<Warning>These KPIs behave differently from rate KPIs.</Warning>

Reducing churn creates a **snowball effect**. Each year the retained base is larger, so the same churn rate saves more in absolute dollars.

The model runs a **two-futures simulation** comparing ARR trajectories with and without improvement:

- `ARR (no fix) = Base ARR × (1 − churn_rate)^year`
- `ARR (with fix) = Base ARR × (1 − improved_churn)^year`
- **COI = cumulative difference over the horizon**

This makes churn and NDR particularly powerful KPIs for retention-focused products.

### Cost Reduction: Two Modes

Throughput KPIs (like Tickets Resolved) can frame impact two ways:

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Reduce Cost" icon="arrow-down">
    Same output, lower budget. The impact shows up as **dollar savings** against the relevant budget line.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Increase Capacity" icon="arrow-up">
    Same budget, more output. The impact shows up as **additional units or throughput** without increasing spend.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

The seller selects which mode fits the customer's situation. Both use the same underlying math but frame the output differently.

### Multi-KPI Compounding

When a supercase covers multiple KPIs that move together (for example Pipeline Value × Win Rate), the combined dollar impact is larger than the sum of the individual gains. Supercase surfaces this **compounding amount** as a separate line so the math reconciles cleanly:

```
Joint impact = Pipeline alone  +  Win Rate alone  +  Compounding
```

The compounding line is detected automatically based on how the KPIs share inputs - no manual configuration required.
