After signing in, you'll land on the **Configuration** wizard - a 5-step setup that builds your business case engine. You can jump between steps freely, but working through them in order gives the best results.

---

## Step 1: Company Information

Tell us who the business case is about.

| Field | What to Enter |
|-------|--------------|
| **Company Name** | The prospect or customer's legal name |
| **Domain** | Their primary website (auto-fills from your email if blank) |
| **Industry** | Their sector - used to pull relevant benchmarks |
| **Employee Count** | Approximate headcount |
| **Annual Revenue** | Approximate annual revenue in USD |

These basics feed into every downstream calculation - revenue drives the financial pool, industry shapes benchmark comparisons.

<Tip>Get these right first. The AI generation in the next step uses this data to research the company automatically.</Tip>

---

## Step 2: Your POV

Define the problem you're solving and why it matters.

This is the heart of Supercase. You're capturing the **Point of View** - the narrative that frames every business case you generate.

**What to fill in:**
- **Problem statement** - What's broken or underperforming for the customer?
- **Fix / end state** - What does "good" look like once the problem is solved?
- **Audience** - Who inside the organization is affected?
- **Key insights** - What differentiates your approach?

**Optional but powerful:**
- **Upload supporting documents** - sales decks, call notes, emails. Supercase extracts the text and uses it as context.
- **Generate with AI** - Hit this button and Supercase will research the company's domain, combine it with any uploaded documents, and pre-fill the entire form. Review and edit the output.

<Note>The AI generation works best when you've completed Step 1 and uploaded at least one document with customer context.</Note>

---

## Step 3: Market Context

Add industry benchmarks and proof points that strengthen your case.

**Master toggle:** Turn "Enable Market Context" on to inject industry data into content generation.

When enabled, you can:
- **Select intelligence packs** - Pre-built collections of industry benchmarks and data points. Toggle on the ones relevant to your market.
- **Upload custom research** - Drop in PDFs of analyst reports, market studies, or competitive intel. Supercase chunks and indexes them automatically.
- **Review and approve chunks** - Each uploaded document is broken into individual data points. Approve the ones you want used, reject the rest.

<Tip>You don't need to upload everything at once. You can come back and add more intelligence packs as you find useful research.</Tip>

---

## Step 4: Buy vs. Build

Model what it actually takes to solve the problem - with or without you.

This step has **three tabs**:

### Project Plan
Define milestones and timelines for both DIY and Vendor approaches. Use **"Generate Generic Project Plan"** to start from a template, then customize.

### Approach & Risks
Describe the risks and consequences of each path. Focus on what can go wrong, not just the task list.

- **DIY Approach & Risks** - What happens when the customer tries to build this internally? (Auto-generates from your POV if left blank)
- **Vendor Approach & Risks** - What does implementation look like with your solution?

<Note>Both fields auto-generate using your POV context and milestones. Review and edit - the AI gives you a starting point, not a final answer.</Note>

### Budget
Enter cost line items for DIY and Vendor approaches. Use **"Generate Generic Cost Calculator"** for a template.

This is where the business case gets concrete: what does each path actually cost? The model uses these costs to calculate net impact and payback period.

---

## Step 5: Cost of Inaction

Configure the financial model that powers every business case.

This is where you define the **KPI mechanics** - how the gap between current state (S0) and achievable state (S1) translates into dollars.

Three mechanism types are available:

| Type | Use When | Example |
|------|----------|---------|
| **Rate Change** | A percentage metric needs to improve | Win rate from 20% to 28% |
| **Throughput Change** | Output volume needs to increase | Deals processed per quarter |
| **Ratio Improvement** | An efficiency ratio needs to shift | Cost per acquisition |

Set your performance gap parameters and adoption rates. The model calculates the rest - including confidence ranges from 1,000 Monte Carlo simulations.

<Tip>See [Cost of Inaction (COI)](/sales/cost-of-inaction) for a deep dive on how S0, S1, and adoption rates work together.</Tip>

---

## You're Done

After completing all five steps, you'll see the **completion screen**. From there:

- **Create your first Supercase** - Jump straight into generating a business case
- **Go to Dashboard** - Review your configuration or start exploring

You can return to any step at any time from the Configuration page to refine your inputs as you learn more from customer conversations.
